The Bangladesh Army originated from the heroic East Bengal Regiment revolts during the 1971 Liberation War. It has since grown into a vital national pillar, balancing internal defense, massive infrastructure projects, disaster management, and prestigious global peacekeeping missions.Explore the stories of the Bangladesh Army through these key phases:The Birth (1971): The story began on March 25, 1971, when the East Bengal Regiment revolted against the Pakistan Army to fight for national independence. The provisional government structured this fight into 11 geographical sectors. Discover the bravery of Bangladesh's highest military honorees on the War Heroes Gallery.Nation Building: The army’s role extends far beyond borders. Soldiers act as the "architects of prosperity" by building bridges, highways, and infrastructure in remote areas like the Chittagong Hill Tracts, while stepping in to manage severe natural disasters.Global Peacekeepers: Bangladesh is consistently among the top contributors to the United Nations peacekeeping missions. Their stories are defined by global diplomacy, bringing stability to conflict-torn nations worldwide.Recent Years: Operating as a strike force to aid civil power, the army recently oversaw complex national elections and navigated major socio-political transitions.
